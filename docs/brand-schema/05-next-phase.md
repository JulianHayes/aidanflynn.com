## Prompt 12: Moat Analysis and Defensibility

Next Phase, Run 1 of 4. Build defensibility.

| Setting | Value |
|---|---|
| Conversation | New conversation or continuation after Prompts 9b+10 |
| Web Search | OFF |
| Deep Research | OFF |
| Output | 6-8 page Word document scoring seven moat mechanisms |
| Time | 120 minutes |

### Context

```text
You have completed the main 11-prompt sequence (Prompts 1-11).

Now evaluate the business against seven moat mechanisms to understand where defensibility is strong and where it is weak.
```

### Prompt

```text
Evaluate Brand Schema against seven moat mechanisms. For each, score as strong/weak/absent and explain what would need to change to strengthen it.

Moat 1 (Proprietary Data): Does the business generate data through its operations that becomes more valuable over time and that competitors cannot replicate? What data could it collect that it currently does not? Example: a database of brand language performance in AI discovery, tied to revenue outcomes.

Moat 2 (Switching Costs): Once a client is using the service, how painful is it to leave? What would make it harder to leave without being manipulative? Example: integrations with their systems, trained staff, muscle memory.

Moat 3 (Network Effects): Does each new client make the service more valuable for existing clients? If not, could a product or community layer create this?

Moat 4 (Expertise Moat): Is there knowledge inside the business that takes years to build and cannot be hired away? What would make the founders' expertise more embedded in systems rather than in their heads?

Moat 5 (Regulatory/Compliance Lock-In): Does the service help clients meet a regulatory requirement that creates ongoing dependency? How deep is that dependency?

Moat 6 (Brand and Reputation Moat): At what point does Brand Schema become the recognised authority in its category? What specific actions accelerate that?

Moat 7 (Process and Methodology Moat): Is there a proprietary method that produces better results than alternatives? Is it documented, named, and protectable?

After scoring, answer three questions:
1. Which single moat mechanism has the highest potential for this business and what is the 12-month plan to build it?
2. Which mechanism is the most dangerous to ignore?
3. If a well-funded competitor entered this space tomorrow, which of these seven would stop them?
```

## Prompt 13: Business Value Drivers and Valuation Levers

Next Phase, Run 2 of 4. Increase enterprise value.

| Setting | Value |
|---|---|
| Conversation | Same conversation as Prompt 12 |
| Web Search | OFF |
| Deep Research | OFF |
| Output | 6-8 page Word document scoring seven value drivers |
| Time | 120 minutes |

### Context

```text
You have analysed moat mechanisms (Prompt 12).

Now evaluate what drives valuation of this business from the perspective of a potential acquirer or investor three to five years from now.
```

### Prompt

```text
Evaluate Brand Schema against seven value drivers.

Value Driver 1 (Revenue Quality): What percentage of revenue is recurring vs. project-based? What shifts the mix toward recurring? (Target: move from 20% recurring today to 50%+ in Year 3.)

Value Driver 2 (Client Concentration Risk): If the top client left, what percentage of revenue disappears? At what number of clients does this risk become acceptable? (Target: no single client > 15% revenue.)

Value Driver 3 (Founder Dependency): Could this business operate for six months if both founders stepped back? What needs to be systematised, documented, or hired for to make that possible?

Value Driver 4 (Intellectual Property): What assets does the business own that have value independent of the founders? Are they protected? (Example: the "After the Screen" paper, proprietary audit methodology, verbal identity codification framework.)

Value Driver 5 (Market Position): Is the business a category leader, a challenger, or one of many? What would it take to be the default choice in its category?

Value Driver 6 (Growth Trajectory): Is revenue growing, and is the growth rate accelerating or decelerating? What is the most capital-efficient way to grow faster?

Value Driver 7 (Strategic Value): Who would benefit most from acquiring this business, and why? What would make it worth more to an acquirer than its standalone revenue suggests? (Example: WPP acquiring Brand Schema for its methodology and founder expertise; AI platform integrating the audit as a freemium feature.)

After scoring, identify five actions over the next 12 months that would most increase the business value in order of impact. Specify: the action, the value driver it strengthens, the expected impact (£ or % increase).
```

## Prompt 14: Demand Validation — Interview Design

Next Phase, Run 3 of 4. Real market testing.

| Setting | Value |
|---|---|
| Conversation | New conversation or continuation |
| Web Search | OFF |
| Deep Research | OFF |
| Output | 4-6 page Word document with 12-question buyer discovery interview |
| Time | 120 minutes |

### Context

```text
You have completed the main launch sequence.

Now design a structured buyer discovery interview to validate demand assumptions made in Prompts 2, 3, and 6.
```

### Prompt

```text
Design a 12-question buyer discovery interview for three buyer archetypes: Heads of Brand, Heads of Content, CMOs at financial services firms.

The interview must test:

1. Problem Recognition: Does the buyer recognise the AI visibility problem without being told it exists?

2. Urgency Perception: How urgent is this problem relative to other priorities? What would make it more urgent?

3. Budget Availability: Is budget available for a solution like Brand Schema? Where would it come from? (New, shifted, combination?)

4. Competitive Awareness: What solutions have they evaluated already? Why rejected? What are they currently using?

5. Decision Timeline: If they decided to solve this, what is the likely decision and implementation timeline?

6. Willingness to Pay: What would they pay for an AI visibility diagnostic? What would they pay for verbal identity codification?

7. Proof Requirements: What proof would make them credible to their executive sponsors?

8. Switching Drivers: What would cause them to leave their current provider (if they have one)?

9. Organisational Barriers: Who else needs to be convinced? What would their concerns be?

10. Implementation Readiness: How much internal resource would they need to allocate?

11. Success Metrics: How would they measure success? What metrics matter most?

12. Relationship Model: Would they prefer to work with a specialist consultancy, add a layer to their agency, or build in-house?

After the interview template, build a scoring rubric for each answer (high demand signal / neutral / low demand signal) and a data collection template for running 15 interviews (five of each buyer type) over a 30-day period.
```

## Prompt 15: Financial Reality Stress Test

Next Phase, Run 4 of 4. Cash and burn modelling.

| Setting | Value |
|---|---|
| Conversation | New conversation or continuation |
| Web Search | OFF |
| Deep Research | OFF |
| Output | 6-8 page Word document with cash flow scenarios and burn rate analysis |
| Time | 120 minutes |

### Context

```text
You have completed the main launch sequence and are entering live operation.

Now model financial reality across stress-case scenarios.
```

### Prompt

```text
Deliver three major stress-test outputs.

Output 1 (Cash Burn Analysis): For each founder at minimum viable salary (£2,500/month), calculate monthly burn including: software tools, insurance, accounting, events, travel, contingency. At zero revenue, how long can the business sustain with combined cash reserves? What is the personal runway for each founder?

Output 2 (Three-Case Revenue Scenarios): Model three cases (pessimistic: 2 clients in Year 1; base: 5 clients; optimistic: 10 clients) showing: monthly cash position, when breakeven is achieved, when founders can increase salary to target (£10,000/month each), cumulative cash gap through Month 18.

Output 3 (Sensitivity Analysis): Identify five variables that most impact cash survival. For each, calculate: if this variable changes 10% (better or worse), how does it affect the breakeven month and total capital injection needed? Variables: Tier 2 pricing, Tier 2-to-Tier 3 conversion rate, sales cycle length, delivery cost per engagement, time to first sale.

After analysis, answer: at what point must the founders secure external capital or supplementary income? What signals would indicate that the pessimistic case is developing (and that a pivot to Audit-only or a hire is necessary)?

Include specific milestones: "If no signed Tier 2 engagement by end of Month 4, founders should reduce scope to Tier 1 Audit only" or similar trigger points.
```
