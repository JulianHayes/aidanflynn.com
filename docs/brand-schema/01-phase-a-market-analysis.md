## Prompt 1: Competitive Landscape and Gap Map

Phase A, Run 1 of 5. Foundation. Do not skip.

| Setting | Value |
|---|---|
| Conversation | New conversation within Brand Schema project |
| Web Search | ON |
| Deep Research | OFF |
| Output | 8-12 page Word document |
| Time | 60 minutes |

### Context

```text
Brand Schema is a specialist AI-augmented verbal identity consultancy. Core: verbal identity codification for AI-mediated brand discovery (GEO/AEO).

Founders: Julian (methodology), Justin (commercial). Background: American Express, Aviva, Alter Domus.

Five capability areas: (1) Verbal identity codification, (2) GEO/AEO strategy, (3) Conversational persona design, (4) Brand governance in AI era, (5) AI visibility diagnostics (lead product).

Sonic and spatial branding are partnership territories.
```

### Prompt

```text
Build a competitive landscape for Brand Schema. Map every competitor across five capability areas.

Output:
1. Competitive grid: five archetypes (brand consultancies, GEO agencies, platforms, conversational AI, accessibility), named competitors, capabilities (full/partial/absent).
2. Narrative per archetype: business model, capability profile vs Brand Schema, why they cannot add missing capabilities, likely response to Brand Schema's growth.
3. Gap map: how many of ten possible capability pairings are integrated by any existing provider? Which gaps largest and most defensible? Which closest to being filled?
4. Defensibility: is white space genuine? Which competitor poses greatest risk? How many months before most likely competitive threat?

Use web search. Do not guess.
```

## Prompt 2a: Demand Signals, Budget Migration, Collision Zones

Phase A, Run 2 of 5. First half of demand investigation.

| Setting | Value |
|---|---|
| Conversation | New conversation within Brand Schema project |
| Web Search | ON |
| Deep Research | OFF |
| Output | 6-10 page Word document |
| Time | 90 minutes |

### Context

```text
Brand Schema positioned at intersection of verbal identity codification and GEO/AEO strategy.

Target sectors: financial services, B2B technology, automotive, retail, luxury, FMCG. FS and B2B strongest beachhead.

Core thesis: brands lacking distinct verbal identity are invisible to AI engines.

Market triggers: AI search adoption, CMO dissatisfaction, budget migration from traditional search, agentic commerce acceleration.
```

### Prompt

```text
Conduct five investigations to determine if genuine demand exists.

Investigation 1 (Demand Signals): Search for evidence that CMOs, Heads of Brand aware of AI visibility problem and actively seeking solutions. Look for CMO surveys, conference talks, job postings, analyst reports. Note date, source, confirmation level.

Investigation 2 (Budget Migration): Where does Brand Schema service budget come from? New budget, shifted budget, combinations? Map three most likely budget sources and estimate difficulty to capture.

Investigation 3 (Collision Zones): Identify three specific buyer situations where pain becomes urgent (e.g., "A retailer discovers product ranks #7 on ChatGPT but #2 on Google"). How frequently? Which sectors most often?

Investigation 4 (Switching Drivers): Why would customer leave current provider (brand consultancy, GEO agency, platform) for Brand Schema? What triggers dissatisfaction? What would they need to see? What's too expensive/complicated/risky?

Investigation 5 (Competitive Awareness): Are brand consultancies, GEO agencies, platforms aware of gap and moving into it? Published content on AI brand visibility? Hiring for GEO + brand strategy? Major consultancies announcing AI brand offerings?

Provide findings in prose with citations. Flag unverifiable claims.
```

## Prompt 2b: Audience Shifts, Regulatory Drivers, Synthesis

Phase A, Run 3 of 5. Second half of demand investigation.

| Setting | Value |
|---|---|
| Conversation | Same conversation as Prompt 2a |
| Web Search | ON |
| Deep Research | OFF |
| Output | 6-10 page synthesis document |
| Time | 90 minutes |

### Context

```text
You have completed Prompt 2a (demand signals, budget migration, collision zones).

Now synthesise with regulatory demand drivers.

Three regulatory frameworks most relevant to Brand Schema:
1. EU AI Act Article 50 (transparency for AI-generated content, effective August 2026)
2. UK sector-led AI regulation and ASA expansion
3. European Accessibility Act and WCAG 2.1 AA compliance

Assess how these create compliance-driven (vs. discretionary) demand.
```

### Prompt

```text
Conduct three investigations into regulatory demand and synthesise with 2a findings.

Investigation 1 (EU AI Act Article 50): Research Article 50 requirements for transparency in AI-generated content. Answer: when does it take effect? Who is covered? What compliance obligations apply to marketing, brand content, conversational AI? How many UK and EU organisations need to comply? Is any brand consultancy, GEO agency, or platform offering Article 50 services? What governance and documentation needed? What percentage of target sectors will need Article 50 help?

Investigation 2 (Accessibility Regulation): Research European Accessibility Act and UK Online Safety Act as they apply to AI-generated content. When do these take effect? How do they intersect with AI brand visibility and conversational personas? What compliance falls on brands vs. platforms vs. agencies? Who currently offers compliance guidance?

Investigation 3 (Regulatory-Driven Demand): Synthesise 2a findings with regulatory drivers. Of the three most likely budget sources identified in 2a, which are accelerated by compliance requirements? Is there "compliance budget" (mandatory) separate from "innovation budget" (discretionary)? Which sectors most vulnerable to regulatory requirements? What is timing difference between discretionary demand (2-3 year cycle) and compliance-driven demand (8-12 month window)?

End with synthesis: is regulatory compliance more reliable demand driver than discretionary buyer awareness? Should go-to-market prioritise compliance angles first?
```

## Prompt 3a: Market Sizes and Underserved Spend

Phase A, Run 4 of 5. Market quantification.

| Setting | Value |
|---|---|
| Conversation | New conversation within Brand Schema project |
| Web Search | ON |
| Deep Research | OFF |
| Output | 6-8 page Word document with tables and sourced analysis |
| Time | 120 minutes |

### Context

```text
Brand Schema positioned at intersection of three capability areas: verbal identity codification, GEO/AEO strategy, conversational persona design.

You have completed Prompts 1 (competitive landscape) and 2a+2b (demand analysis).

Target sectors: financial services, B2B technology, automotive, retail, luxury, FMCG.
```

### Prompt

```text
Size the market for Brand Schema's services.

Output:
1. Global addressable market estimate: GEO/AEO market size (analyst reports), brand strategy/consultancy market, conversational AI market, intersection of these three (your addressable market). Provide low-mid-high ranges with citations.
2. UK-specific market share: What percentage of global market is UK? What is approximate GBP value of UK intersection market?
3. Underserved spend estimate: Of total intersection market, what percentage is currently underserved (no integrated provider)? Base on Prompt 2 competitor gap findings. Provide low-mid-high estimates with reasoning.
4. Sector breakdown for UK market: Allocate total UK market across six target sectors based on ad spend, AI adoption pressure, compliance urgency. Show working for each sector.
5. Claims register: Every numeric claim must carry citation. Flag anything you could not verify with source. Flag conflicts between sources with explanation.

Do not estimate. Search and cite. If number is not sourced, flag as unconfirmed.
```

## Prompt 3b: Scenarios, Switching Drivers, Unit Economics

Phase A, Run 5 of 5. Financial grounding.

| Setting | Value |
|---|---|
| Conversation | Same conversation as Prompt 3a |
| Web Search | ON |
| Deep Research | OFF |
| Output | 8-12 page Word document with tables and unit economics |
| Time | 120 minutes |

### Context

```text
You have completed Prompt 3a (market sizing).

Now build scenario modelling, switching drivers, and unit economics to ground the market sizing in financial reality.
```

### Prompt

```text
Deliver four major outputs.

Output 1 (Scenario Maths): Apply low/mid/high scenario percentages to your 3a market sizes. For each scenario (low 12%, mid 25%, high 30% underserved), calculate global USD and UK GBP intersection market underserved values. Model separately any compliance-driven demand overlay ($120M-$240M globally tied to EU AI Act, European Accessibility Act, UK ASA/Online Safety Act). Justify mid-case 25% using evidence from 2a and 2b findings.

Output 2 (Switching Drivers): Build two tables. Table 1: why brands leave traditional brand consultancies for AI-era specialists (7 drivers: machine-unreadable guidelines, absent GEO/AEO, CMO dissatisfaction, homogenisation risk, regulatory urgency, misaligned fees, missing conversational persona). Table 2: why brands leave GEO/SEO agencies for integrated identity consultancies (6 drivers: brand-voiceless GEO, single-engine focus, no brand depth, agentic commerce demands, conversion value of AI referrals, information gain needs).

Output 3 (Unit Economics): Build framework for two-person London-based consultancy. Six project types with fee ranges benchmarked against IBISWorld, Clutch, published agency pricing. Three retainer service lines. Detailed annual cost structure (approximately £211,860 total). Two breakeven calculations: survival breakeven at ~£265K revenue, reasonable-salary breakeven at ~£377K.

Output 4 (Claims Register): List all numeric claims from both 3a and 3b with citations. Classify as: confirmed, training data, unconfirmed, or derived. Include confidence levels.
```
