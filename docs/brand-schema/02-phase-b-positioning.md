# Phase B — Positioning and Validation

> Part 3 of 6 in the Brand Schema Master Prompt Library

Three prompts testing positioning against reality through expert panel and stress testing.

---

## Prompt 4a: Advisory Panel — Independent Assessments

Phase B, Run 1 of 3. Expert validation.

| Setting | Value |
|---|---|
| Conversation | New conversation within Brand Schema project |
| Web Search | OFF |
| Deep Research | OFF |
| Output | 8-12 page Word document with three independent assessments |
| Time | 90 minutes |

### Context

```text
You have completed Phase A (Prompts 1-3). You have established competitive landscape, demand signals, and market sizing.

Now test positioning through expert panel. Simulate three independent advisors evaluating Brand Schema:

1. Elena: Brand strategist, 15+ years. Evaluates founder distinctiveness, white space fit, positioning territories, reactions to name "Brand Schema," conceptual framework.

2. Marcus: Tech entrepreneur and VC. Evaluates revenue targets, what "AI-augmented" means operationally, commercial viability, risks, business model type, co-founder dynamic.

3. Priya: CMO of FTSE 250 multi-brand group. Evaluates from buyer perspective: likelihood of meeting, credible switching drivers, proof points needed, procurement barriers, whether positioning resonates.
```

### Prompt

```text
Generate three independent Phase 1 assessments from Elena, Marcus, and Priya.

Each panellist should provide:
- Their honest assessment of founder distinctiveness and credentials
- Whether they believe the white space is real
- Their view on the positioning and naming
- Critical gaps they see
- What proof points or case studies would convince them
- Their confidence level in the founders' ability to execute

Elena focus: positioning territories and conceptual coherence.
Marcus focus: revenue model, financial viability, co-founder dynamics.
Priya focus: buyer credibility, switching drivers, procurement realities.

Do not flatter. Base assessments on evidence from Prompts 1-3.

Constraints: CVO (conversational voice/operations) offering is excluded. Sonic and spatial remain partnership territories. This is about Tier 1 and Tier 2 verbal identity services.

Stop at end of Phase 1 independent assessments. Do not proceed to challenge round or convergence yet.
```

## Prompt 4b: Advisory Panel — Challenge Round and Converged Recommendation

Phase B, Run 2 of 3. Positioning refinement.

| Setting | Value |
|---|---|
| Conversation | Same conversation as Prompt 4a |
| Web Search | OFF |
| Deep Research | OFF |
| Output | 8-10 page Word document with challenge round and converged recommendation |
| Time | 90 minutes |

### Context

```text
You have completed Phase 1 independent assessments from Elena, Marcus, and Priya.

Now conduct Phase 2 (challenge round) and Phase 3 (converged recommendation).
```

### Prompt

```text
Phase 2 (Challenge Round): Each panellist critiques the other two. Elena challenges Marcus and Priya on buyer credibility vs. financial projections. Marcus challenges Elena on whether "bothness" (verbal identity + GEO) is actually deliverable or conceptual waffle. Priya challenges both on whether CMO is the right buyer or whether problem is really owned by Head of Content/Head of SEO.

Phase 3 (Converged Recommendation): After challenge round, produce a single converged recommendation covering:

1. Positioning recommendation: Which of the three positioning territories (The AI Brand Readiness Firm / The Verbal Identity Codification Specialist / The GEO Integration Specialist) should Brand Schema lead with? Why?

2. Value proposition: What is the single most differentiated claim Brand Schema can make that the panel actually believes?

3. Go-to-market priorities: What should the founders prioritise in first 90 days to overcome the credibility gaps the panel identified?

4. Proof point strategy: What case studies or proof points would most accelerate buyer credibility?

5. Risks and prerequisites: What must be true for this positioning to work? What would kill it?

6. Confidence assessment: Overall, on a 1-10 scale, do we believe this business will find product-market fit?
```

## Prompt 4c: Positioning Stress Test Against Competitive Landscape

Phase B, Run 3 of 3. Positioning validation.

| Setting | Value |
|---|---|
| Conversation | Same conversation as Prompt 4b (or new conversation with Prompts 1 + 4b referenced) |
| Web Search | OFF |
| Deep Research | OFF |
| Output | 6-8 page stress test document |
| Time | 120 minutes |

### Context

```text
You have established competitive landscape (Prompt 1) and received panel recommendation (Prompt 4b).

Now stress-test the recommended positioning against competitive reality.
```

### Prompt

```text
Conduct six-part stress test of Brand Schema's positioning.

Part 1 (Competitor-by-Competitor): For each major competitor archetype (brand consultancies, GEO agencies, big consultancies, marketing platforms), explain why the panel's positioning recommendation is vulnerable. What specific moves could each competitor make to neutralise Brand Schema's advantage?

Part 2 (Hostile Cross-Examination): Assume a sceptical buyer. Challenge the "only we claim" from the panel recommendation. What would a buyer say is wrong with that claim? What counter-evidence exists?

Part 3 (Buyer Objection Simulation): Role-play three buyer types (CMO, Head of Content, VP Digital) encountering Brand Schema positioning. What objections would each raise? How credible are those objections?

Part 4 (Timing and Market Readiness): The panel assumed AI discovery momentum continues. What if AI discovery plateaus or consolidates around 2-3 dominant platforms? How does that change the positioning urgency?

Part 5 (Break the Positioning): Describe three scenarios where the positioning completely fails despite the panel's confidence.

Part 6 (Overall Scorecard): Score the positioning 1-10 on: clarity, credibility, defensibility, market timeliness, founder fit, scalability. Explain each score. Provide overall positioning viability score.
```
