## Prompt 9a: Six Hats Analysis

Phase D, Run 1 of 4. Comprehensive stress test.

| Setting | Value |
|---|---|
| Conversation | New conversation within Brand Schema project |
| Web Search | OFF |
| Deep Research | OFF |
| Output | 10-14 page Word document with all six hats |
| Time | 150 minutes |

### Context

```text
You have completed Phases A-C: market validation, positioning stress test, service architecture, messaging framework.

Now apply Six Thinking Hats to stress-test the entire proposition.
```

### Prompt

```text
Deliver all six hats in full.

White Hat (Facts): What claims are presented as facts but are actually unvalidated assumptions? (Year 1 revenue projections, 40% AI-augmentation efficiency gain, conversion rates from diagnostic to strategic engagement). Flag what has been tested in market vs. what is theoretical.

Red Hat (Intuition/Feeling): What is your gut reaction to this business as presented? Does it read like an established firm or a zero-client startup? Is the volume of strategic documentation functioning as a substitute for selling? Is Justin's role narrative credible?

Black Hat (Critical Judgment): Five failure modes with probability estimates. For each: what would need to be true for this to fail? How likely is that condition?

Yellow Hat (Positive Thinking): What is the single hardest-to-replicate asset? What is the most effective sales tool? What combination of factors, if they align, makes this business unstoppable?

Green Hat (Creative): What would the founders do that is unconventional and powerful? (Product-led growth? Annual UK AI Brand Visibility Index? Regulatory compliance positioning?)

Blue Hat (Process): What is the dominant process flaw? What is the most significant mutual blind spot? Are there false dichotomies in the current strategy?

Conclude with: five significant concerns from the full analysis. Rate overall viability (0-100%). Recommend specific actions to increase viability.
```

## Prompt 9b: Pre-Mortem and Consolidated Recommendations

Phase D, Run 2 of 4. Failure analysis and next steps.

| Setting | Value |
|---|---|
| Conversation | Same conversation as Prompt 9a |
| Web Search | OFF |
| Deep Research | OFF |
| Output | 8-12 page Word document with three failure narratives and recommendations |
| Time | 120 minutes |

### Context

```text
You have completed Six Hats analysis (Prompt 9a).

Now conduct pre-mortem: imagine the business has failed by March 2027. What happened?
```

### Prompt

```text
Deliver three distinct failure narratives, each 200-300 words:

Narrative 1 (Market Failure): Demand existed but the founders bypassed it due to misaligned buying triggers, slower-than-expected AI search adoption, or budget pressure. They built a product nobody was buying. Timeline: by month 8.

Narrative 2 (Execution Failure): Over-planning, co-founder misalignment under financial stress, scope creep on first engagement, no automation of audit process. They built something good but couldn't deliver it at the speed/efficiency required. Timeline: by month 12.

Narrative 3 (Competitive Failure): An incumbent brand consultancy (WPP, Accenture) entered the space with trust, distribution, and bundled pricing. The standalone market collapsed. Timeline: by month 14.

After narratives, produce consolidated recommendations:

Part A (Five Critical Risks with Mitigations): For each risk, specify: the risk, the trigger event that signals it's real, the mitigation action.

Part B (Five Untested Assumptions with Cheap Validation): For each assumption, specify: the assumption, why it matters, a cheap way to test it before full launch.

Part C (Service Architecture Changes): Based on Six Hats and pre-mortem, what should change in the service architecture (Prompt 5)?

Part D (Messaging Changes): Based on Six Hats and pre-mortem, what should change in the messaging (Prompts 7a, 7b)?

Part E (Kill Criteria Reminder): What are the three kill criteria most important to define now?

Part F (Confidence Score): Overall viability score (0-100%). What would need to be true to raise the score above 70%?
```

## Prompt 10: 90-Day Launch Plan

Phase D, Run 3 of 4. Go-to-market roadmap.

| Setting | Value |
|---|---|
| Conversation | New conversation within Brand Schema project |
| Web Search | ON |
| Deep Research | OFF |
| Output | 12-16 page Word document with week-by-week plan, pipeline targets, budget, success metrics |
| Time | 120 minutes |

### Context

```text
You have completed stress testing (Prompts 9a + 9b).

Now build a 90-day launch plan (April-June 2026) that operationalizes everything you've learned.
```

### Prompt

```text
Deliver five major outputs.

Output 1 (Week-by-Week Operational Plan): Weeks 1-13 showing: marketing actions, sales activities, infrastructure build, content production, metrics tracking. Assume Good Friday (3 April) and Easter Monday (6 April) reduce the two-week window from 10 to 8 working days. One primary task per founder per day max.

Output 2 (Pipeline and Revenue Targets): Specific targets for GBP invoiced by Day 90 (recommend £30K-£60K) and GBP pipeline (recommend £80K-£150K). Ground in B2B professional services benchmarks (sales cycle length, conversion rates, deal velocity for new consultancies).

Output 3 (Go-to-Market Strategy): Multi-channel approach: thought leadership, event attendance, targeted outreach, partnership development. Specify: which channels, weekly cadence, measurable output per channel.

Output 4 (Infrastructure Checklist): Two-week checklist for: Companies House formation, business bank account, insurance, software tools, website, proposal templates, sales enablement materials.

Output 5 (Success Metrics and Decision Framework): Leading indicators (weekly sign-ups, inbound inquiries, decision-stage pipeline). Lagging indicators (monthly revenue, conversion rates, customer acquisition cost). Three-scenario decision framework at Day 90 (proceed, recalibrate, kill).

Specify revenue targets and success criteria that address the 58% viability gap identified in Prompt 9b. What must be true by Day 90 to increase viability above 70%?
```

## Prompt 11: Content Strategy and Thought Leadership

Phase D, Run 4 of 4. Content and awareness plan.

| Setting | Value |
|---|---|
| Conversation | New conversation within Brand Schema project |
| Web Search | ON |
| Deep Research | OFF |
| Output | 14-18 page Word document with 28 content pieces, 12-week calendar, distribution plan |
| Time | 150 minutes |

### Context

```text
You have built the 90-day launch plan (Prompt 10).

Now build thought leadership strategy that extracts maximum commercial value from the "After the Screen" research paper.
```

### Prompt

```text
Deliver five major sections.

Section 1 (Content Atomisation): From the "After the Screen" research paper, extract 28 derivative content pieces (short posts, articles, guides, case study frameworks, tool templates) mapped across six thematic seams: citation economy and AI search shift, brand homogenisation risk, verbal identity gap, regulatory pressure, sonic and spatial identity, technical structuring/schema.

Section 2 (Distribution Channels): Identify ten named trade publications (Marketing Week, Campaign, The Drum, Search Engine Journal, etc.), ten named podcasts, eight professional communities, and a prioritised conference calendar (April-June 2026 UK events).

Section 3 (Conversion Mechanics): Explain the core principle: data gifts replace meeting requests. Build a 48-hour follow-up sequence (first email, second email if no response, call timing). Link explicitly to the Brand Schema Audit Tier 1 service.

Section 4 (12-Week Content Calendar): All 28 pieces scheduled to specific weeks, channels, and founder profiles. Weeks 1-12 showing: piece title, format, distribution channel(s), owner (Julian/Justin), CTA (all point to diagnostic).

Section 5 (Measurement Framework): Weekly leading indicators (content published, engagement rate, email opens, click-through rate), monthly lagging indicators (inbound inquiries, pipeline value from content source, lead-to-client conversion).

Flag the highest-value content action: the "UK AI Brand Visibility Index" (Week 8) is the biggest time investment but likely highest ROI. Requires decision: should it cover four target sectors (financial services, B2B tech, automotive, retail) or go broad for press coverage? Has the 40-60 brand selection been decided?
```
